Rehabilitation & Hydrotherapy

Canine Rehabilitation at the Good Dog Spa provides the same high quality care we expect for ourselves after an injury. Working closely with Wigram Vet, we create individualised programs to get your pet back on all four paws.

Expert Care for Recovery & Wellness

Rehabilitation is a vital part of recovery following an orthopedic, musculo-skeletal, or neurological injury or event. By working closely with our team at Wigram Vet, Kate will provide an individualised program to get your pet back on all four paws again.

Our rehabilitation services are beneficial for a wide variety of pets to improve their mobility and quality of life.

Hydrotherapy ‚Äì Underwater Treadmill

The AQUATREAD canine underwater treadmill provides a controlled environment for the physical rehabilitation and conditioning of canine patients.

Buoyancy Benefits

Water minimises weight-bearing on joints and reduces stress on tissues, allowing more comfortable exercise.

Muscle Strengthening

Water resistance strengthens and tones muscles while improving cardiovascular fitness.

Pain Management

Water pressure decreases pain receptor activity. Warm water stimulates endorphin release for natural pain relief.

Customisable

Adjustable speed, water levels, inclines, and water jets create challenges tailored to each patient.

Proper Gait

Encourages upright position, building confidence with normal posture and gait patterns.

Faster Recovery

Promotes earlier return to normal function after injury or surgery.

Kate Donald

Canine Rehabilitation Practitioner

Kate is a Canine Rehabilitation Practitioner with 7 years experience in the industry. She completed her BSc in Biology and Animal Physiology in 2013. Since then, she has been working in Veterinary clinics in Christchurch and Dunedin performing in-clinic and in-house rehabilitation.

She is currently enrolled at the University of Tennessee and completing her certification in Canine Rehabilitation while working from Wigram Vet and Good Dog Spa.

Kate treats pets with orthopaedic and spinal injuries, both post-operative and conservative management cases. As well as conditions such as hip and elbow dysplasia, osteoarthritis, and degenerative myelopathy. She also treats injured working dogs, competition agility dogs and helps older pets gain an improved quality of life.
